Understanding Each Platform

RightSpend: Commitment Free Discounts

What Makes It Unique

RightSpend pioneered Commitment Free Discounts (CFDs), a proprietary technology that delivers Reserved Instance-level savings (25-55%) without any lock-in period. Using AI-powered Convertible RI optimization, RightSpend continuously adjusts coverage based on real-time usage patterns.

Unlike traditional RIs or Savings Plans that lock you into 1-3 year commitments, RightSpend gives you the flexibility to cancel anytime while still achieving significant savings. This is ideal for companies with unpredictable growth, seasonal workloads, or those avoiding capital commitments.

Best Use Cases

  • Startups and scale-ups with unpredictable growth trajectories
  • Companies with seasonal or variable EC2 workloads
  • Organizations avoiding long-term cloud commitments
  • Companies wanting to augment existing Savings Plans/RIs without additional lock-in

Pricing

25% of net new savings. No upfront costs, no hidden fees. 90-day money-back guarantee if no measurable savings achieved.

ProsperOps: Autonomous Commitment Management

What Makes It Unique

ProsperOps focuses on fully autonomous management of your AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud commitment portfolios. Rather than just making recommendations, ProsperOps takes action on your behalf, buying and managing Savings Plans and Reserved Instances to maximize your Effective Savings Rate (ESR).

The platform is "set it and forget it" - you configure your risk tolerance and coverage targets, and ProsperOps handles all the commitment purchasing, optimization, and lifecycle management automatically. They also offer workload optimization through ProsperOps Scheduler for additional savings.

Best Use Cases

  • Enterprises comfortable with 1-3 year cloud commitments
  • Teams lacking resources to manage commitments manually
  • Multi-cloud environments (AWS, Azure, GCP)
  • Organizations wanting maximum discount rates via upfront RIs

Pricing

Gainsharing model (percentage of savings). Pricing not publicly disclosed - contact for quote.

nOps: Comprehensive Cloud Cost Platform

What Makes It Unique

nOps takes a comprehensive platform approach to AWS cost optimization, offering multiple features beyond just commitment management. Their suite includes Compute Copilot (spot instance optimization), ShareSave (commitment management with risk-free guarantee), nOps Essentials (rightsizing, scheduling, idle resource removal), and Business Contexts+ (cost allocation).

This makes nOps ideal for teams that need a one-stop-shop for all AWS cost optimization activities. The platform is ML-powered and manages over $2B in cloud spend, recently ranking #1 in G2's cloud cost management category.

Best Use Cases

  • Teams needing spot instance optimization alongside commitments
  • Organizations wanting comprehensive cost visibility and allocation
  • Companies with oversized resources needing rightsizing recommendations
  • Enterprises needing multi-dimensional cost optimization (rate + usage + waste)

Percentage of savings. Pricing described as "fair" and "transparent" by customers. Contact for detailed quote.

Common Questions

Can I use multiple platforms together?

Yes, but it's typically not recommended. Using multiple commitment optimization tools can lead to overlapping coverage and reduced efficiency. However, you could combine a commitment tool (like RightSpend) with a broader platform's non-commitment features (like nOps' rightsizing or cost allocation).

Which platform saves the most money?

It depends on your specific situation. ProsperOps and nOps can theoretically deliver higher discount rates (up to 72%) via upfront RIs, but require lock-in. RightSpend delivers 25-55% without lock-in. The "best" option depends on your risk tolerance, workload predictability, and total cost of ownership (including wasted commitments).

What if my AWS spend is under $100K/month?

All three platforms can work for smaller AWS spends, but economics matter. With gainsharing pricing, even small accounts can benefit. RightSpend and nOps have been known to work with companies spending $50K+/month. ProsperOps may have higher minimum requirements - contact them directly.

How do I switch from one platform to another?

Switching depends on existing commitments. If you're using ProsperOps or nOps and have active RIs/Savings Plans, you'll need to wait for those to expire or accept the cost of selling them in the RI Marketplace. With RightSpend's commitment-free approach, you can switch at any time with just 30 days' notice.
